About Me

I was a former high schooler at Wake Early College of Information and Biotechnologies, studying both at WECIB and Wake Technical Community College, where I learned how to turn code into control and structure into freedom.

After being dealt a bad hand at the immigration desk, where paperwork decided my future instead of my work ethic, I had to pause my formal education in the States in 2025 and pick up my education in India. The transition was rough, but that setback only fueled my passion for self-learning and personal growth. My path is by no means linear, and now I'm honestly struggling to find my way back into my passions. There are a lot of uncertainties and not many things I do know, but what I what I can tell you is this: I'm not giving up.

My path in computer science was never driven by trying to be more competent than others, and that it was solely my internal drive keeping me going, and now, more than ever, I am going to have to prove that. From long runs to late nights, I’ve learned that consistency puts luck in your favor, and the result of relentless effort under pressure will bring just that.

I’ve earned certifications like Python ITS, PCAP, CompTIA Network+, and Cisco Certified Support, but credentials only tell part of the story. What truly matters is how I use them to build tools, algorithms, and frameworks that bring order to complexity, even when the stakes are high.

My current focus is on computer science, data structures, and automation, with one goal in mind: to engineer systems that amplify human discipline.

If you’re working on something that demands focus under pressure, tests conviction, and refuses mediocrity, please reach out.

Because in this world, the only thing that can turn coal into diamonds is pressure. So come join me on this journey of transformation.
