Ikigai

You strive to do great things, and let's say you accomplish that. You do it, so much so, that victory becomes the standard. This makes victory, not a cause for celebration, but the minimum level of acceptable performance. Anything less becomes unsatisfactory, and is deemed a failure.

Congratulations. You may become very successful. You might also become very miserable.

In my life, it resonates with virtually everything I do. From running to schooling to developing. I chose to put in the work not because I had a good work ethic, not because I was obliged to, but because I was competent.

The teammates I trained with got faster, and I couldn't afford to fall behind in a sport where you can't catch up.

The peers I learned with grinded LeetCode day in day out, and I wasn't going to let them beat me in a technical interview.

And even the people I didn't fully know, like the LinkedIn warriors, I wasn't going to sit there and watch them have their own spotlight.

The point being, YOU can't afford to fall behind, because the moment you step into the ring with lethargy you have lost the battle. Don't put yourself in a spot where you know you won't grow because it's a decision that no one can make for you, and a decision that no one can save you from.

In the end, you'd only be harming yourself, and if you can't even understand that, you have bigger fish to fry brochaco.
